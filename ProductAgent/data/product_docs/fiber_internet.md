# Fiber Internet Products — Business Knowledge Base

## Overview

Our Business Fiber Internet product line delivers symmetrical, dedicated fiber-to-the-premises (FTTP) connectivity over a 100% fiber-optic network. All fiber products use FTTP (Fiber To The Premises) technology — fiber runs all the way to the customer's building, with no shared coax or copper in the last mile. This architecture is fundamentally different from HFC (cable) or DSL and provides inherently superior performance and reliability.

**Product IDs covered:** FIB-1G, FIB-5G, FIB-10G

---

## Business Fiber 1 Gbps (FIB-1G)

### Description
Symmetrical 1 Gbps fiber internet designed for small to medium-sized businesses. Both upload and download run at 1 Gbps simultaneously, making it ideal for businesses that transfer large files, host cloud applications, conduct video conferencing, or support a distributed workforce.

### Technical Specifications
- **Download speed:** 1 Gbps (1,000 Mbps)
- **Upload speed:** 1 Gbps (1,000 Mbps) — fully symmetrical
- **Technology:** FTTP (Fiber To The Premises)
- **Latency:** Typically 5–15 ms to regional data centers
- **Jitter:** < 2 ms under normal load

### Uptime SLA

- This translates to a maximum of approximately **8.7 hours of planned or unplanned downtime per year**
- SLA credits are issued for outages exceeding the monthly window

### Hardware Included
- Business Gateway router (wireless-capable, with 4 × GbE LAN ports)
- Optical Network Terminal (ONT) installed by our technician during installation
- Free professional installation and on-site configuration

### Use Cases — Best Fit
- Small to medium offices (10–50 employees)
- Businesses with cloud-first IT strategy (Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Salesforce)
- Video conferencing-heavy teams (Zoom, Teams, WebEx)
- Retail locations with POS systems, surveillance, and guest Wi-Fi
- Professional services firms (legal, accounting, consulting)

### Common Customer Questions
**Q: Why do I need symmetrical speeds?**
A: Most cable (HFC) internet is asymmetrical — e.g., 1 Gbps download but only 50–100 Mbps upload. For businesses that upload large files to the cloud, share screens in video calls, or run hosted servers, limiting upload to 50 Mbps creates a serious bottleneck. Symmetrical fiber eliminates that constraint entirely.

**Q: What happens when the fiber is cut?**
A: In the event of a physical fiber cut, our network operations center is automatically notified and dispatches a technician. For sites requiring redundancy, we recommend pairing FIB-1G with a secondary coax or mobile failover link.

**Q: Is installation disruptive?**
A: Installation typically takes 2–4 hours. The technician runs the fiber drop to the demarc point, installs the ONT, and configures the Business Gateway. Most businesses can stay operational during installation.

**Q: My team uses a 10 GbE internal LAN — will 5 Gbps fiber be a bottleneck?**
A: Not materially for most workloads. Internal LAN speeds deal with server-to-server traffic; external internet traffic almost never saturates a 5 Gbps WAN link unless you have a very specific bulk-transfer use case. If that's the case, the 10 Gbps tier (FIB-10G) is available.

A: Yes. The 5 static IPs included are a /29 block. Additional /28 or /27 blocks are available via our offer management team.

## Fiber vs. Coax — When to Recommend Fiber

| Factor | Fiber (FTTP) | Coax (HFC) |
|---|---|---|
| Upload speed | Symmetrical (equal to download) | Asymmetrical (10–20% of download) |
| Latency | 5–15 ms | 15–40 ms |
| Uptime SLA | 99.9%–99.95% | 99.5% |
| Shared infrastructure | Dedicated fiber strand | Shared neighborhood node |
| Best for | Upload-heavy, latency-sensitive, high SLA needs | Cost-sensitive, download-heavy, light upload |

Recommend fiber when a customer mentions:
- Video conferencing as a primary communication mode
- Cloud backup or large file uploads
- VoIP with quality SLA requirements
- Multi-site SD-WAN deployments requiring consistent WAN performance
- Any regulatory compliance requirement with uptime mandates
